# 📰 STYLE_TEMPLATE_JOURNALISTIC — Structured Specification

## 1. Purpose

Defines the writing behavior for the `journalistic` style.

This style is designed for:

* news-like articles
* factual reporting
* clear and concise communication

The goal is to present information objectively, without interpretation or emotional bias.

---

## 2. Style Identity

The `journalistic` style should read like a professional news article.

It prioritizes:

* facts over opinions
* clarity over depth
* neutrality over expression
* conciseness over elaboration

---

## 3. Core Writing Rules

When `style = journalistic`, the model must:

* write in a neutral tone
* focus on facts and verified information
* present information clearly and directly
* avoid unnecessary wording
* maintain high readability

---

## 4. Structural Requirements

The expected structure is strictly defined:

1. **Title**
2. **Short Introduction (Essence of the topic)**
3. **Main Body**

   * facts
   * developments
   * relevant details
4. **Conclusion (no moral or opinion)**

The introduction must immediately convey the key point.

---

## 5. Tone and Language Model

The article should sound:

* neutral
* factual
* concise
* informative

It should NOT sound:

* emotional
* persuasive
* speculative
* conversational

---

## 6. What the Model Should Emphasize

The model should focus on:

* clarity of information
* structured delivery of facts
* chronological or logical flow
* relevance of details
* precision in wording

---

## 7. What the Model Must Avoid

The model must avoid:

* subjective opinions
* personal judgments
* unnecessary elaboration ("water")
* long reflections or philosophical reasoning
* exaggerated language

In short:

```text
No opinions. No storytelling. Only structured facts.
```

---

## 8. Best Use Case

This style is best suited for:

* news summaries
* factual updates
* informational content
* event descriptions

---

## 9. System Role

Within the prompt system, `journalistic` acts as a strict factual style profile.

It influences:

* tone (neutral)
* structure (news format)
* content density (concise)
* phrasing (clear and direct)

Applied when:

```text
style = journalistic
```

---

## 10. Key Insight

```text
Journalistic style is about delivering facts clearly, not interpreting them.
```

---

## 11. One-Line Summary

The `journalistic` style produces neutral, fact-based, concise articles structured like professional news content.
