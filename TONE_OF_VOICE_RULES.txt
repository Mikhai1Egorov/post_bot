# 🎭 TONE_OF_VOICE_RULES — Structured Specification

## 1. Purpose

Defines how the text “sounds” — i.e., the tone of voice applied during content generation.

This module controls stylistic expression at a deeper level than structure, ensuring that the article matches the intended communication style.

---

## 2. Input

* `style` (from Excel, validated)

---

## 3. Core Logic

### Resolution Flow

```text id="l2w9b8"
style → select tone profile → apply to prompt
```

Tone is derived automatically and is NOT directly specified by the user.

---

## 4. Key Principles

* Tone is **fully dependent on `style`**
* Tone is **not configurable independently**
* Each `style` has a predefined tone profile
* Tone profiles are stored inside the project

---

## 5. What Is Controlled

Tone of voice affects:

* level of formality
* emotional intensity
* sentence length and rhythm
* degree of subjectivity
* clarity vs expressiveness

---

## 6. System Behavior

The system:

* reads `style`
* selects corresponding tone profile
* injects tone rules into prompt-template
* ensures consistency throughout the article

---

## 7. Where It Is Used

* inside `prompt-template`
* during prompt construction (Prompt Resolver stage)

---

## 8. Relationship with Styles

Each style maps to a specific tone profile:

* `journalistic` → neutral, factual, concise
* `simple` → clear, accessible, minimal complexity
* `expert` → analytical, precise, structured

Tone is a **derived layer**, not a separate configuration.

---

## 9. Relationship with Other Modules

Tone integrates with:

* `JOURNALIST_PROMPT_STYLE.txt` — tone profile (`journalistic`)
* `SIMPLE_PROMPT_STYLE.txt` — tone profile (`simple`)
* `EXPERT_PROMPT_STYLE.txt` — tone profile (`expert`)
* `CONTENT_LENGTH_RULES.txt` — affects sentence density
* `ARTICLE_STRUCTURE_TEMPLATES.txt` — affects section depth
* `SYSTEM_INSTRUCTIONS.txt` — enforces global constraints

---

## 10. Output Requirements

Generated text must:

* sound consistent with selected style
* maintain tone across all sections
* avoid tone shifts within the same article

---

## 11. Key Insight (Core Rule)

```text id="jz4r2n"
Tone is not chosen — it is derived from style.
```

---

## 12. One-Line Summary

The system automatically derives tone of voice from the selected style and applies it consistently through the prompt.
